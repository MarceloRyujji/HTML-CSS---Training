    Scientific article: Sustainable fuels: comparison between JET-A1 and electro fuels

    Scientific article: Sustainable fuels: comparison between JET-A1 and electro fuels

    In this article, we will study the feasibility and impacts, both economic and environmental, of using electrofuels as fuel for aircraft. Due to its extremely high energy coefficient, it can be viable for electric and high-range aircraft, a way to reduce environmental pollution and make the air mode even more accessible, clean and economical. The main objective is to study the viability of the project, what would be the possible next substitute for aviation fuel, both its storage, transportation and its technical and economic bias, how the system would work, its application and, if possible, an estimate of the start of the operations. One thing to highlight is because it is the first article in Brazilian Portuguese on this topic and with this application, the importance of this study is to see the viability and evolution of human technologies, abandoning fossil fuels and using renewable fuels. The main problem is its feasibility in engineering, in addition to the urgency due to the critical condition of the planet, how adaptation would be carried out on aircraft, how the material would be collected and mainly the value of the entire process, from extraction to use and how this value would be passed on. To carry out the research, we will use the field of electrical technologies, studies conducted by Airbus and data from missions and uses of electrofuels.

    Scientific article: The importance of preparing and applying PLEM/PRAI emergency plans, debris removal logistics: case study in general aviation

    Scientific article: The importance of preparing and applying PLEM/PRAI emergency plans, debris removal logistics: case study in general aviation

    This study will address the importance of developing and applying emergency plans to debris removal logistics with the aim of carrying out a descriptive and exploratory approach to the topic, together with a case study of a real accident, in the context of general aviation. From this article it is possible to observe that anticipating some type of occurrence, the administrator must develop and keep the PLEM/PRAI, this document needs to consider the measures to be taken and those responsible, in the event of an incident or accident within or even a few nautical miles outside the limits of the airport. This work was developed based on bibliography, specialized websites and information from bodies related to civil aviation.
